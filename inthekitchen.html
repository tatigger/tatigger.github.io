In The Kitchen

In general I eat relatively low-carb. It makes my life easier. Salads are a nearly daily part of the strategy. Pictured above is some lettuce that I harvested from my garden. Recipes can be hard to evaluate, although there are many available online and in magazines and books. Recipes and packaged foods that are "low-carb" are often higher in fat. Be selective about which fats and amounts that you use. Here I include some recipes that I enjoy.

Paleo Coconut Flour Zucchini Bread
3/4 c coconut flour 6 eggs, room temperature
1/2 c maple syrup 1/4 c coconut oil, melted
1 t vanilla extract 1 t ground cinnamon
1/4 t ground nutmeg 3/4 t baking soda
1/2 t xanthan gum 1/4 c chocolate chips
1 1/2 c zucchini, shredded, tightly packed
Preheat oven to 350F. Line 9x5 inch loaf pan with parchment paper or line muffin tin with 12 baking cups.
Combine all ingredients except zucchini and chocolate chips in large bowl. Mix well, breaking up lumps. Batter will be thicker than expected of quick breads. Stir in shredded zucchini and combine well. Scoop batter into pan or muffin tin, smooth top.
Bake at 350F for 45 to 50 minutes until top is golden and firm. Bake muffins at 350F for 25 to 30 minutes. Cool in the pan for 15 minutes, then transfer to wire rack to cool.
If desired, drizzle top with melted chocolate chips.
Store in refrigerator for best shelf life. Individual slices or muffins freeze well, defrost in refrigerator.

I made several small adaptations to the original recipe found on The Detoxinista website (recipe linked with the clickable title above). Shredded zucchini needs to have as much moisture as possible squeezed out prior to use. I twist a cup at a time in a thin kitchen towel (like the flour sack type of towel) and squeeze like crazy. I bake at high altitude, but made no adjustments for the altitude. I freeze individual slices or muffins on a parchment-lined sheet, then wrap in each serving in foil and store in a labeled zip-lock bag. Otherwise my 12 muffins would last 5 minutes. Maybe not even that long. Don't forget to add the carbs and fat to the total for the chocolate chips you use.
